<DetailsReveal summary="About the Metro Cable Case Study">

The case study used in this guidebook is a fictional but representative example based on a cable and internet provider. The company, Metro Cable, serves customers in the Kansas City metropolitan area. As with most public utilities, Metro Cable is regulated by a Public Service Commission (PSC) which is responsible for ensuring that Metro Cable provides quality service at reasonable prices to its customers. In return, the PSC grants Metro Cable exclusive rights to provide cable and internet service to Kansas City residents and businesses.

Over the past several years, the cost to provide cable and internet service to Kansas City customers has risen, and Metro Cable forecasts that they will no longer be profitable in the near future. Therefore, Metro Cable intends to apply to the PSC for a rate increase. PSC Rate Case hearings are held infrequently and there is a rigorous process by which the PSC will examine all aspects of Metro Cable operations to determine if a customer increase is warranted. A key question the PSC will strive to answer is whether customers are happy with the service being provided for the money they are spending.

Metro Cable is aware that current Customer Satisfaction ratings have declined over the past several years, and the company fears that poor customer satisfaction will result in their requested rate increase being denied. Therefore, a problem-solving team is assigned the task of determining the root causes of low customer satisfaction scores.

The problem-solving team will use AI tools throughout their efforts to analyze assumptions, determine influencing factors, identify root causes, and propose a set of solutions. Through their initial analysis the team will determine that a key driver of customer satisfaction is the consistent and predictable fulfillment of service appointment requests. Furthermore, the team will discover that late and missed service appointments are related to how appointments are assigned to technicians and the amount of time technicians spend driving from one appointment to another.

Ultimately, the problem-solving team will define a set of solutions to increase appointment predictability. These solutions will be evaluated and prioritized based on feasibility, impact, and business value. Final recommendations will be presented to Metro Cable leadership for budgeting and implementation approval.

Some of the recommended solutions will require enhancements to the Metro Cable systems for technician assignment and routing. AI Tools can be used by system designers and developers to deliver new features and enhancements; however, those practices are outside the scope of this guidebook.

Remember, curiosity and the talent for asking questions are the keys to successful AI-Enabled Problem Solving. Hopefully, the example used in this guidebook will inspire you to leverage AI tools on the problems you are trying to solve.
