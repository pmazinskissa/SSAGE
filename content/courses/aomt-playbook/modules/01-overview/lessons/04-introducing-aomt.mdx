# Introducing AI-Enabled Problem Solving

**AI-Enabled Problem Solving** is a non-linear approach to operational changes that leverages Artificial Intelligence and emerging technologies to move much farther and faster in achieving an organization's objectives.

## Two Core Principles

<Callout type="concept" title="Data First">
Ingest and interrogate the data landscape to reveal and verify evidence-based opportunities. Instead of starting with stakeholder impressions, start with data patterns and validate them against stakeholder experience.
</Callout>

<Callout type="concept" title="AI First">
Design solutions that take full advantage of AI capabilities to make quantum gains in efficiency. Ask "what becomes possible if AI handles this?" rather than "how can AI speed up step 3?"
</Callout>

## Why Organizations Fail to Execute Fast Enough

Most organizations understand the urgency of AI-enabled change — yet the majority fail to capture the value they expect. Execution speed, not strategy, is the limiting constraint.

**The top reasons organizations stall:**

1. **Sequential, waterfall thinking.** Initiatives are designed phase-by-phase — waiting for diagnosis to close before designing, waiting for design to complete before implementing. In fast-moving environments, this creates months of latency between insight and action.

2. **Change management treated as a final step.** Organizations wait until after solutions are built to begin stakeholder engagement, training, and adoption planning. By then, resistance is entrenched and rollout timelines are compressed.

3. **Perfect-before-deploy culture.** Teams over-engineer solutions trying to address every edge case before going live. Iterative deployment with rapid feedback loops consistently outperforms delayed big-bang releases.

4. **AI strategy disconnected from operations.** AI investments are managed by a centralized team with limited frontline involvement. Practitioners closest to the work are not equipped to identify, design, or sustain AI-enabled improvements.

5. **Under-investment in data readiness.** AI cannot operate on fragmented, siloed, or low-quality data. Organizations that delay data infrastructure investment consistently see AI pilots fail to scale.

<Callout type="concept" title="The Performance Gap Is Widening">
The gap between AI leaders and late adopters is not linear — it is compounding. Leaders reinvest efficiency gains into further AI capability, widening their advantage each cycle.
</Callout>

| Metric | AI Leaders | Late Adopters |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Speed to insight from data | Hours to days | Weeks to months |
| Diagnostic cycle time | 2–4 weeks | 8–16 weeks |
| Opportunity identification rate | 3–5× higher | Baseline |
| Cost reduction from AI-enabled ops | 15–30% | &lt;5% |
| Frontline AI adoption rate | 60–80% of workforce | &lt;20% of workforce |

<Callout type="tip" title="Agility as Competitive Advantage">
The organizations that capture AI's full value are not necessarily the ones that invest the most — they are the ones that move fastest, learn from early deployments, and iterate continuously. A six-week diagnostic cycle executed twice beats a perfect four-month analysis every time.
</Callout>
