# AI-Empowered Problem Solving

<CaseStudyBanner>

**Metro Cable** is a fictional cable and broadband services company used as the running case example throughout this module. Reading this summary first will help the specific numbers and scenarios in each lesson feel grounded rather than appearing out of nowhere.

**The Core Problem:** Metro Cable's first-visit resolution rate has declined from **78% to 62%** over the past 12 months. Repeat dispatches are increasing, field technicians are losing productive time, and customer dissatisfaction is rising. With a PSC Rate Case hearing in 12 months, the executive team needs demonstrated improvement quickly.

**Who is the customer?** In this case, both **end subscribers** (awaiting service) and **internal field technicians** (whose routing efficiency and workload are directly affected) are treated as customers.

**What the practitioner team does across Module 2:**

- **Lesson 2.3 — Problem Statement:** Define the problem scope, boundaries, decision makers, and success measures (customer satisfaction ratings, rate case approval)
- **Lesson 2.4 — Issues Tree:** Decompose the resolution rate decline into 5 branches — Dispatch & Routing, Technician Capability, Parts Availability, Job Complexity, and Customer & Scheduling Factors — each with testable hypotheses
- **Lesson 2.5 — Data Request:** Identify and formally request 14 months of dispatch records, technician certification data, geographic zone maps, and customer satisfaction surveys
- **Lessons 2.7–2.8 — Analysis:** Use AI to run correlation analyses between technician travel distance, certification level, and first-visit resolution rates across service zones

</CaseStudyBanner>

This section introduces the core diagnostic and prioritization methodology that sets AI-Enabled Problem Solving apart from traditional consulting approaches. Rather than exclusively relying on stakeholder interviews and assumptions, this approach starts with data and uses AI to accelerate every stage of analysis.

<Callout type="concept" title="Two-Phase Approach">
AI-Empowered Problem Solving operates in two distinct phases. Phase 1 focuses on iterative diagnosis — ingesting data, surfacing patterns, and synthesizing findings. Phase 2 focuses on prioritizing the opportunities uncovered during diagnosis into an actionable roadmap.
</Callout>



## Phase 1: Iterative Diagnosis

Phase 1 follows a structured but non-linear process built around three core activities:

<ProcessFlow steps={[
  { title: "Data Ingestion", description: "Connect to data sources, load structured and unstructured data into AI tools, and establish a working data environment" },
  { title: "AI-Assisted Diagnosis", description: "Use AI to decompose problems, generate hypotheses, run analyses, and validate findings against benchmarks" },
  { title: "Synthesis", description: "Convert validated findings into clearly articulated opportunities with supporting evidence and quantified impact" }
]} />

Each activity feeds back into the others. A finding from diagnosis may trigger a new data request, and synthesis may reveal gaps that require additional analysis cycles.

## Phase 2: Prioritization

Once opportunities are identified and documented, Phase 2 applies structured scoring methods to rank them by value, complexity, and strategic alignment. This transforms a list of possibilities into a sequenced roadmap that stakeholders can review and act on.

<Callout type="tip" title="Why This Matters">
Traditional problem-solving can take weeks of interviews and workshops before a single insight emerges. With AI-empowered diagnosis, practitioners can surface data-driven insights within hours, then validate those insights with stakeholders — dramatically accelerating time to action.
</Callout>

The lessons that follow walk through each part of this process in detail, with practical examples based on a fictional case study involving Metro Cable.
