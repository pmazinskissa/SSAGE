# Curiosity-Enabled Analysis

One of the most transformative aspects of AI-powered analysis is the removal of the cost barrier to curiosity. In traditional consulting, every analysis takes time to prepare, run, and interpret. Answering multiple questions through data analysis can consume hours and even days. With AI, the marginal cost of asking one more question is nearly zero — enabling a style of analysis driven by genuine curiosity rather than budget constraints.

## The Context + Task + Output Framework

To get the best results from AI analysis, structure your prompts using three components:

1. **Context** — Provide the AI with the relevant background. What data is loaded? What has been found so far? What is the overall objective?

2. **Task** — Clearly state what you want the AI to do. Be specific about the analytical method, the variables involved, and any constraints.

3. **Output** — Describe the format you want the results in. A table? A visualization? A narrative summary? Specify the level of detail.

<Callout type="concept" title="Prompt Structure Example">
**Context:** "We have 14 months of Metro Cable dispatch data loaded. Previous analysis showed that first-visit resolution varies significantly by service zone."

**Task:** "Perform a correlation analysis between technician certification level and first-visit resolution rate, controlling for travel distance and job complexity."

**Output:** "Present results as a correlation matrix with p-values, followed by a plain-language interpretation of statistically significant relationships."
</Callout>

## Chaining Analysis Questions

The most powerful insights come from chaining questions — where the answer to one question immediately prompts the next:

- "What is the average resolution rate by zone?" leads to
- "Which zones are below the 70th percentile?" leads to
- "Do underperforming zones share any common characteristics?" leads to
- "Is technician experience level correlated with zone performance?"

<Callout type="tip" title="Follow the Thread">
Do not pre-plan your entire analysis sequence. Start with a broad question, interpret the results, and let your curiosity guide the next question. AI makes this exploratory approach not just possible but optimal.
</Callout>
